01 В· PHASE A вҖ” UNDERSTAND & FRAME

Interpretation of the product strategy

The shop is a trust machine disguised as a store. Customers trade a 7вҖ“14 day wait for a verifiable, product-level price advantage вҖ” a deal that only works if every promise (reference price, delivery clock, compensation, shelf life, approvals) is presented with contractual clarity. The universe is a retention and discovery layer wrapped around that contract; it may make the wait feel purposeful, but it must never make the contract harder to read. The pet profile, enriched progressively, is the asset the shop exists to create.

Customer anxieties (ranked)

  1. Is this product genuine? Counterfeit pet food is a real fear; вҖңSupplier-verifiedвҖқ must be explained, never overstated.
  2. Will it actually arrive? Paying in full for something sourced after payment feels risky. The 14-day firm commitment and 5% compensation are the answer вҖ” show them before payment.
  3. Is the saving real? Reference prices must look auditable: verification date, exact amount, per product.
  4. Will it be fresh? Shelf life is invisible until delivery; the 6-month guarantee and exact expiry after sourcing carry this.
  5. Am I locked in? Free cancellation before sourcing; a visible boundary, never a buried clause.
  6. Will I be charged without asking? Replenishment reserves, never pays. Say so plainly.

Commercial trust moments

  • First product page: price + reference + exact saving + verification date.
  • Purchase-state comprehension: available to order vs reserve now vs unavailable.
  • Pre-payment disclosure block: 14-day clock, cancellation boundary, 5% wallet rule, 3-month credit expiry, shelf-life guarantee.
  • Sourcing confirmation: exact expiry date revealed.
  • Any exception: plain language, explicit action, recorded approval.
  • Day-15 event: compensation appears automatically, already explained.

Experience tensions

  • Immersive narrative вҶ” contractual precision
  • Premium calm вҶ” discount evidence (savings up to ~50%)
  • Warmth toward pets вҶ” adult, non-childish tone
  • Progressive profiling вҶ” frictionless checkout
  • Space metaphor вҶ” Persian plain language for money
HOUSEHOLD

The boundary of ownership: access, addresses, payment, wallet, orders, shared supply. It is administrative вҖ” designed with quiet, systematic surfaces, never the emotional star.

PET PROFILE

The emotional and intelligence center. Every product, mission, estimate, and future service attaches here. Grows one useful field at a time; each field must unlock visible value.

DIRECT COMMERCE

Always one step away. Search, categories, product detail, cart, checkout live on calm, light, conventional surfaces. It is the default path, not the fallback.

OPTIONAL UNIVERSE

A guided discovery and progression layer on dark, atmospheric surfaces. Improves orientation, education, basket building, and the wait вҖ” and always carries a plain exit to direct shop.

Risks of designing this wrong

вҖў Space theme reads childish вҶ’ premium buyers bounce at first impression.
вҖў Narrative tracking implies false operational progress вҶ’ trust collapse on first delay.
вҖў Savings styled like bazaar discounts вҶ’ reference prices look inflated, not audited.
вҖў Mirrored-English RTL вҶ’ forms, steppers, and numbers feel foreign; errors in price reading.
вҖў Universe placed in the purchase path вҶ’ conversion drops; вҖңecommerce with obstacles.вҖқ
вҖў Wallet expiry or cancellation boundary discovered late вҶ’ perceived as fine print traps.
02 В· DESIGN PRINCIPLES

Eight rules that govern every decision

P1
Money is never a metaphor
Prices, payment, cancellation, expiry, compensation, and approvals use plain Persian on conventional surfaces вҖ” even inside the universe.
P2
Two surfaces, one world
Commerce lives on light surfaces; the universe on dark. The surface itself tells the customer which contract applies.
P3
Persian first, not translated
Composition, direction, numerals, and tone are conceived in Persian RTL. Latin appears only where product reality demands it.
P4
Evidence, not exclamation
Savings are shown as audited facts вҖ” amount, percentage, verification date вҖ” never as shouting badges.
P5
The pet is the organizing noun
Products, estimates, missions, and history are framed around the pet by name вҖ” warm, adult, never cartoonish.
P6
Exceptions break character
Normal progress may be narrative. Delay, sourcing failure, required action, or money at risk switches instantly to plain language.
P7
Ask when it pays back
Every profile question must immediately improve a product, estimate, or recommendation the customer can see.
P8
States over decoration
Purchase states, sourcing states, wallet states, and shelf-life states drive the visual system; ornament never encodes status alone.

09 В· MOTION PRINCIPLES

Motion explains; it never performs

System

  • Durations: press feedback 90ms В· state changes 180ms В· sheets/dialogs 320ms В· warp transitions 600ms (universe only, skippable).
  • Easing: decisive ease-out for entrances; symmetric ease-in-out for spatial moves; no bounce or overshoot in commerce.
  • Origin-aware: sheets rise from the trigger edge; detail views expand from the tapped card (shared-element product photo).
  • Interruptible: every transition yields to input immediately; navigation never waits for animation.
  • Loading: skeletons in layout order (rightвҶ’left); no full-screen spinners; progress bars fill rightвҶ’left.
  • Reduced motion: warp becomes crossfade; journey chart becomes stepped states; all meaning preserved without movement.

Frequency rules

  • High-frequency commerce (add to cart, quantity, filters): immediate, вүӨ180ms, no ornament.
  • Checkout: zero decorative motion. Only validation feedback and the payment-state change. Nothing delays the pay action.
  • Order journey: the vessel advances along the plotted route only when a real internal milestone posts; arrival gets the one celebratory moment in the system.
  • Exceptions: exception panels appear with a plain 180ms fade вҖ” deliberately unlike universe motion, signaling a register change.
  • Rewards: a single brass-glint on wallet credit issuance; never confetti.
