GATE 4 · FORMATIVE USABILITY VALIDATION — RESEARCH KIT
Test the promises, not the pixels
Moderated formative study on the Gate 4 research prototype — version-locked build G4-R1 on frozen data gate4-data.v1.js (base 3B.1; neutral care content, no internal placeholders). No new features, rewards or navigation concepts enter during Gate 4; every post-test change must cite observed evidence. Recommended: 6–8 participants (mix: current online pet-food buyers / offline-only buyers; single- and multi-pet households), 45–60 minutes, think-aloud, Persian sessions.
PRIMARY RESEARCH QUESTIONS
Q1 · Do users understand the trust promises — supplier-verified authenticity (identity private, country shown), dated reference-price saving, guaranteed shelf life?
Q2 · Do they understand household inventory vs pet consumption (a bag belongs to the home; eating belongs to a pet)?
Q3 · Do they understand estimates and uncertainty — bands, confidence words, «تخمینی», wider striped bands for «نمی‌دانم»?
Q4 · Can they complete the key flows without explanation?
Q5 · Does the Garden create emotional value WITHOUT being read as a health score or performance grade?
Q6 · Does Today remain useful and calm — nothing demanded on an empty day?
Q7 · Do delayed and unavailable states feel transparent and controllable?
1 · Moderated session guide — neutral by construction
SESSION STRUCTURE — 45–60 MIN
0–5 · Welcome + consent. «هیچ پاسخ درست یا غلطی وجود ندارد؛ ما طرح را آزمایش می‌کنیم، نه شما.» Recording consent; think-aloud demo on a neutral app.
5–12 · Context interview. Pets, current food buying (where/how often/price feel), any tracking habits. No product exposure yet.
12–45 · Tasks 1–7 on the prototype, in order (scenario tabs are moderator-only). Prompt is read aloud verbatim; then silence. Probe only after the participant acts or stalls > 20s.
45–55 · Debrief. Trust/uncertainty/Garden interpretation questions (bank ↙), preference ranking last.
55–60 · Close. Incentive, thanks, data label (P1…P8).

«این باغ دربارهٔ حیوانتان چه می‌گوید؟ اگر یک هفته سر نزنید چه می‌شود؟»

Never say: «آسان بود؟» / «دوست داشتید؟» / feature names before the participant uses them / any explanation of bands, inventory or the Garden before the debrief.
SCREENER · CONSENT · DATA
Screener (5 items): owns ≥ 1 cat/dog they feed personally · has bought pet food in the last 3 months · mix of online + offline-only buyers · at least 3 multi-pet households · excludes UX/market-research professionals and anyone who saw earlier gates.
Consent & recording: written consent before recording; screen + audio only (no face required); participant may pause or withdraw at any moment with no effect on the incentive — stated aloud at the start.
Data retention: recordings anonymized to P-codes within 48h; raw files deleted after analysis sign-off (≤ 30 days); quotes used only with the P-code; contact details stored separately and deleted after the incentive is paid.
DRY-RUN CHECKLIST + PILOT CHECKPOINT
Internal dry-run (before recruiting): □ build shows rev G4-R1 in the footer · □ T1–T7 walked end-to-end on the session device · □ every task start step reachable from the moderator tabs · □ «نمی‌دانم» and «سهم را می‌گویم» land on different screens · □ «به من خبر بده» creates no order · □ no internal placeholder or approval-pending text visible anywhere · □ recording + timer tested.
Pilot (P0, one participant): run the full protocol; afterwards review prompt comprehension, task order, session length and sheet usability. Only wording/logistics fixes allowed before freeze; any prototype change re-runs the dry-run checklist and bumps the rev (G4-R2). P0 data is excluded from findings.
RULES OF EVIDENCE
✓ A finding requires an observed behavior or verbatim quote — «felt like» notes are tagged impression, not finding.
✓ Severity is judged against task goal, not aesthetics.
✓ Prototype limits (static fields, simulated buttons) are logged as N/A, never as failures.
✓ Garden misreadings (health score, ranking, obligation) are always logged verbatim — they answer Q5.
✓ Policy questions participants raise (compensation, cancellation, refunds) are recorded for the Product/Ops register — the moderator answers verbatim: «سؤال را یادداشت کردم؛ در این نمونه اطلاعات بیشتری وجود ندارد. لطفاً با آنچه می‌بینید ادامه دهید.» — never that the policy is still being designed.

4 · Findings template + evidence-based revision list
A · USABILITY DEFECTS
Interaction or comprehension failures against a task goal.
Entry: ID · task · evidence (Pn + quote/behavior) · frequency /8 · severity (blocker / major / minor) · affected screen(s).
Fix path: design revision in Gate 4.1, retest if blocker.
B · CONTENT PROBLEMS
Wording misread, tone missteps, terms not understood («انبار خانه», «تخمینی», «اصالت»…).
Entry: term/copy · misreading (verbatim) · frequency · proposed rewording · approval owner (content / vet where applicable).
Fix path: copy revision; expert-gated wording goes to the approval pipeline, never straight to screens.
C · PRODUCT-POLICY GAPS
Questions design cannot answer: compensation, cancellation after sourcing, refunds, replacement, sourcing failure.
Entry: question asked (verbatim) · trigger moment · participant expectation · frequency.
Fix path: routed to the Product/Ops register (Gate 3B §6) — no design invention.
D · OPTIONAL PREFERENCES
Likes/dislikes with no task impact (colors, avatar style, wanting more Garden objects…).
Entry: preference · who · context. Explicitly non-binding — acted on only if it also appears as A or B evidence.
